# Whose Ball: product thesis

> One page. Provisional until ship; revisit once, at ship time. Assembled 2026-07-13 from George's mined motivation framework (`docs/brainstorms/2026-07-13-motivation-framework-ore.md`) and the six ratified build decisions. Every claim here is his; arrangement only.

## The question this product answers

What conditions help capable people repeatedly turn intention into shipped work, especially when the work is difficult, voluntary, social, and time-constrained? The platform is the practical expression of the answer.

**The thesis: make meaningful progress easier to see than unfinished work, and make the next useful action easier to choose than avoidance.**

This is not a better container for tasks. It is an operating system for turning intention into shared, visible, verifiable progress.

## Target behavior (the one number that matters)

Increase the percentage of participants who always know, and complete, their next shippable action.

## Design principles (five conditions, each shipped as a mechanic)

- **Meaning.** Every project answers who benefits, what changes when it ships, and what done looks like. Captured once, at the Define stage, where it is useful; never as a tax on tasks.
- **Agency.** The platform never assigns or commands. People claim work, choose their approach, and set their own commitments. Constraints are legitimate; control is not.
- **Mastery.** Track what capability was demonstrated, not just what closed. Feedback is actionable ("your setup requires an undocumented environment variable"), never a verdict ("needs work").
- **Momentum.** The basic unit is the next shippable movement, not the task. Movement is celebrated; stillness gets a question, not a judgment.
- **Mutuality.** The social consequence of work is visible without surveillance: who is waiting on you, who you unblocked, who needs help. Recognition follows contribution, assistance, learning, and shipping, never raw volume.

## Core workflow

- **Projects travel a six-stage trajectory: Define → Commit → Build → Verify → Ship → Teach.** Quality and knowledge transfer are stations of the work, not cleanup after done. Tasks ride a light flow inside (todo / building / verifying / done); ceremony lives at the altitude where narrative exists.
- **The ball is a button.** Every project carries exactly one named next action and one person holding it. On your command center it is clickable, dropping you directly into where the work lives. Optional when-commitments attach when you want the extra push and expire quietly when they pass.
- **Define gates the story, never the work.** Tasks can be created, assigned, and moved regardless of stage. A project that is moving while undefined gets a nudge, not a lock.
- **Blocked is honest, visible work.** Entering it asks what you tried, what you need, and who can unblock you; it surfaces on the momentum board and on the unblocker's own dashboard. Asking for help is productive work, not an admission of failure.
- **Personal command center is the front door**: what matters most now, your next concrete action, what is blocked, who is depending on you, with the cohort's pulse in a strip one glance away.
- **The cohort view is momentum, not rank**: collective tiles and a shipping feed of real movements. Movement stays public; comparison does not.

## Anti-features (as binding as the features)

No activity leaderboards. No points or badges as the motivation system: visualize progress, do not manufacture importance. No status reporting rituals: updates emerge from real actions (commits, PRs, deploys, reviews, blocker changes). No treating all stalled work the same: the intervention must match the cause. Progress, not applause.
